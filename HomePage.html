    The Rusty Spoon: A Honest Review

    9999 views

    11-12-2024 17:19

    The Rusty Spoon

    127 Maple Hollow Road, Asheville, North Carolina, USA

    Marcus

    @brunchking94

    Hands down one of the best brunch spots I’ve had the pleasure of discovering in the Blue Ridge area. The Rusty Spoon brings together the comfort of mountain dining with the artistry of a gourmet kitchen and the result is unforgettable. The smoked trout benedict? A revelation. Perfectly balanced with rich, velvety hollandaise and tender, flavorful trout, it was plated with the kind of detail that made me pause before diving in (but not for long). The sourdough waffles had this airy crunch that played beautifully with the warmth of the maple and the creaminess of whipped butter — a dreamy balance of texture and flavor. The space itself is cozy and refined — think modern cabin meets design-forward bistro. Soft lighting, plush seating, and warm wood tones make it feel both inviting and special. Whether you're seated by the window or tucked into one of their curved booths, it's a place you want to settle into. Their coffee game is clearly taken seriously. My latte came out with delicate latte art and a deep, smooth roast that held its own without overpowering. And if you’re into bold flavors, don’t miss their whipped feta with chili oil and fresh herbs it’s colorful, creamy, and just the right amount of heat. From the thoughtful plating to the warm service, everything about The Rusty Spoon feels intentional. If you’re a fan of rustic comfort food elevated with a chef’s touch, this place should be at the very top of your list.
